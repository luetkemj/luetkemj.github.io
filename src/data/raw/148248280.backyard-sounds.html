[one who does not read]

It was the end of 2022 when I decided to try and read more. It wasn't long before I noticed an unexpected trickle of water flowing down a dry river bed. A long winter was coming to an end. An unexpected spring thaw quickly turned into what felt like a hundred year flood.

I felt more creative energy than I had in years. I hadn't been that productive in over a decade. It was all so easy.

Until it wasn't.

I was so focused on the product, I had lost all sight of the practice. To make more time to make more stuff I read less and less. The river slowed down and eventually dried up. There I stood, on the banks of a dusty river bed, as baffled about the current state of affairs as I was about how it had ever gotten going in the first place.

Its clear now, having started reading again, how fundamental it is to the practice. It doesn't take much to get the river flowing. But it does take a steady stream of input.

Some recommendations from my own summer reading:

  • Make It Till You Make It - Brendan Leonard

  • The Witches - Roald Dahl

  • This Is Water - David Foster Wallace

  • Ducks - Kate Beaton

  • Atomic! - Henry Kuttner
